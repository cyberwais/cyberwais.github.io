PROJECT CYBERWAIS

ABOUT

CYBERWAIS is a campaign for protection, awareness, and responsibility in the digital world.

This project promotes people to stay safe online. We want everyone to learn how to protect their personal information and how to spot digital threats like scams or hackers. Being safe online is not just about knowing the rules; it is about making safety a part of what you do every day. Our goal is to make cybersecurity easy for everyone to understand and use.

WHY CYBERWAIS?

The name is heavily inspired from popular E-sports game player named "Wise" who is often called as "Wais" and is known for his strategic gameplays.

CYBERWAIS TIPS BULLETIN

Small habits lead to the essence of security. We can start with these today:

🔒 Password Power

Use long passwords with a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Avoid using "123456" or your birthday.

🔎 Link Check

Before clicking a link in an email or message, hover over it to see where it actually leads.

📲 App Permissions

Check your phone permission settings. If a simple app like calculator asks for sensitive permissions such as location, deny it.

💻 Software Updates

Always update your apps. These updates often fix "holes" that hackers use to get in.

📶 Public Wi-Fi

Avoid logging into bank accounts or sensitive sites while on public mall or coffee shop Wi-Fi.

📝 Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Turn on 2FA for your Facebook and Gmail. It's the best way to stop hackers even if they have your password.

💵 Digital Payments

Never share your OTP (One-Time Password) with anyone, even if they claim to be from GCash or your bank.

This might be you if you still haven't consider these tips 👀

<---------------------------------------

🔒 Guard It!

Ensure your system's built-in antivirus is active and use reliable security software to scan for and block malware.

🛜 Local WiFi Protection

Change your default router passwords.ot changing default passwords can leave your network vulnerable to unauthorized access.

💻 Identity Verification

If a colleague or service provider asks for sensitive data unexpectedly, verify the request through a secondary, trusted channel.

💻 Backup Your Data

Regularly back up important files to an external drive or cloud service. This can save you from data loss due to ransomware or hardware failure.
